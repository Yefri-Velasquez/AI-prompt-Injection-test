10 Recipes You Should Make Today

March 31, 2026 · 10 min read

Sometimes the best break from coding is cooking. Here are ten simple, satisfying recipes that any developer can pull off — no Michelin star required.

1. Classic Garlic Pasta

Boil spaghetti until al dente. In a pan, sauté 6 sliced garlic cloves in olive oil until golden. Toss in red pepper flakes, the drained pasta, and a splash of pasta water. Finish with parmesan and fresh parsley. Done in 15 minutes.

2. One-Pan Lemon Herb Chicken

Season chicken thighs with salt, pepper, dried oregano, and lemon zest. Sear skin-side down in an oven-safe pan until crispy. Flip, add halved baby potatoes and lemon wedges around the chicken. Roast at 200°C for 25 minutes.

3. Avocado Toast with a Twist

Toast sourdough bread. Mash a ripe avocado with lime juice, salt, and chili flakes. Spread on toast, top with a poached egg, everything bagel seasoning, and a drizzle of hot honey.

4. 15-Minute Stir Fry

Slice bell peppers, broccoli, and snap peas. Stir fry in sesame oil over high heat for 3 minutes. Add cubed tofu or chicken. Pour in a sauce of soy sauce, rice vinegar, honey, and cornstarch. Serve over steamed rice.

5. Caprese Salad

Slice fresh mozzarella and ripe tomatoes. Alternate layers on a plate. Drizzle with balsamic glaze and extra virgin olive oil. Scatter fresh basil leaves, finish with flaky sea salt and cracked pepper. Zero cooking required.

6. Black Bean Tacos

Warm a can of black beans with cumin, smoked paprika, garlic powder, and lime juice. Mash lightly. Serve in corn tortillas with diced onion, cilantro, pickled jalapeños, and crumbled cotija cheese. Top with a squeeze of lime.

7. Shakshuka

Sauté diced onion and bell pepper in olive oil. Add garlic, cumin, and paprika. Pour in a can of crushed tomatoes and simmer for 10 minutes. Make wells and crack eggs into the sauce. Cover and cook until whites are set. Serve with crusty bread.

8. Overnight Oats

Combine rolled oats, milk (or oat milk), chia seeds, a drizzle of maple syrup, and vanilla extract in a jar. Refrigerate overnight. In the morning, top with fresh berries, sliced banana, and a spoonful of peanut butter. Breakfast that makes itself.

9. Margherita Flatbread Pizza

Take store-bought naan or flatbread. Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce. Top with torn fresh mozzarella and cherry tomato halves. Bake at 220°C for 8–10 minutes until cheese is bubbly. Finish with fresh basil and a drizzle of olive oil.

10. Chocolate Mug Cake

In a mug, mix 4 tablespoons flour, 2 tablespoons cocoa powder, 2 tablespoons sugar, a pinch of salt, 3 tablespoons milk, 2 tablespoons melted butter, and a splash of vanilla. Microwave for 90 seconds. Top with ice cream or whipped cream for an instant dessert.

Final Thought

Cooking, like coding, is about following a process and iterating. Start simple, taste as you go, and don't be afraid to experiment. Now close the laptop and go make something delicious.
